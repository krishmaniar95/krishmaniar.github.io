Student Consultant

In my first semester at Northeastern University, through my professor, Professor Himlona Palikhe, I got a chance to work with the CEO of ImEx Cargo on a cargo loading optmization and visualization project.
The project required our team to research and suggest an optimal solution for loading cargo onto standard freight sizes.
Through our research we were able to suggest a first fit and best fit alogrithm to tackle the problem.
The second part of the project was to be able to visualize this fitting so the workers would be able to see in real time how to load the cargo.
This was a complicated task that was difficult to accomplish in the time frame we had, so we decided to divert our efforts into finding open source tools that would be doing this. Through our research we found an Excel macros tool that perfectly solved the visualization problem.
We presented our suggestions to the CEO and walked through the Excel solver workbook that took care of the visualization of cargo loading.

Co-Founder

At MPSTME, along with few of my classmates I started a team that to participated at Formula Student, India. As a founding member, I wore many hats, some of which included heading design for the drivetrain, recruiting and training new members for the team. I heavily involved myself in all aspects of design, sourcing and manufacturing as well as finding funding to make our dream to race our car at the competition come true. Here I attach a bit more about the competition directly from the organizers:
"Formula Bharat is an Indian Formula Student-style competition. The purpose of the competition is to promote the awareness of practical engineering experience and student innovation through creating a competitive Indian platform for Indian and International teams alike.
Teams take on the assumption that they are a manufacturer developing a prototype to be evaluated for production and a group of developers are interested in investing in their project. The intended sales market is the non-professional weekend autocrosser. Therefore, the car must have very high performance in terms of its acceleration, braking and handling qualities. The car must be low in cost, easy to maintain and reliable. In addition, the car’s marketability is enhanced by other factors such as aesthetics, comfort and use of common parts. In short, would you, the weekend autocrosser, be interested in purchasing one of these vehicles?
The challenge to the design team is to design and fabricate a prototype that best meets these goals and intents. Over the course of a five day competition, a jury of experts from the motorsport, automotive and supplier industries judge the design, cost and business planning of all the teams to determine the best team and vehicle; in addition the team’s on-track performance scores will demonstrate how well they hold up under real life conditions.
The Formula Bharat competition will be divided into 2 categories – Static and Dynamic – which account for a total of 1000 points."
